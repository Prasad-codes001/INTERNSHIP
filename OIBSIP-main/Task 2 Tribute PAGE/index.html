Mahatma Gandhi

1869 - 1948

The Father of the Indian Nation

Mahatma Gandhi - Leader of the Indian independence movement

Introduction

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, widely known as Mahatma Gandhi, was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule. He inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.

"Be the change that you wish to see in the world."

Early Life

Gandhi was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India. He studied law in London and went to South Africa in 1893 to represent an Indian merchant in a lawsuit. It was in South Africa that Gandhi first employed nonviolent resistance in a campaign for civil rights.

Philosophy of Nonviolence

Gandhi developed his philosophy of "Satyagraha" (truth force), a nonviolent way of protestation. He believed that nonviolence required more courage than violence. His approach to political and social change through nonviolent civil disobedience inspired countless movements worldwide.

Gandhi during the famous Salt March in 1930

Key Movements

1917: Champaran Satyagraha

Gandhi's first major civil disobedience movement in India supporting oppressed farmers.

1930: Salt March

A 24-day march to produce salt from seawater, defying British salt laws.

1942: Quit India Movement

A decisive movement demanding an end to British rule in India.

Legacy

Gandhi's birthday, October 2nd, is celebrated worldwide as the International Day of Nonviolence. His principles influenced notable figures like Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela. Gandhi remains an enduring symbol of peace, nonviolence, and simple living.

"An eye for an eye only ends up making the whole world blind."
